Chocolate Chip Cookies

Created by Betty Crocker Kitchens

  • 2 1/4 cups of Gold Medal™ all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of softened butter
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup of packed brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla
  • 2 cups of semisweet chocolate chips
  1. Heat the oven to 375° Fareneheit. In a small bowl, mix flour, baking soda and salt; set aside.
  2. In large bowl, beat softened butter and sugars with electric mixer on medium speed, or mix with spoon about 1 minute or until fluffy, scrapping side of bowl occassionally.
  3. Beat in egg and vanilla until smooth. Stir in flour mixture just until blended (dough will be stiff). Stir in chocolate chips.
  4. Onto ungreased cookie sheets, drop dough by rounded tablespoons 2 inches apart.
  5. Bake 8 to 10 minutes or until light brown (centers will be soft). Cool 2 minutes; remove from cookies sheet to cooling rack. Cool completely, about 30 minutes. Store covered in airtight container.

I enjoy this recipe because I love sweets and cookies are one of my favorite desserts.

I especially enjoy them when they are still warm from the oven and with a glass of milk.
